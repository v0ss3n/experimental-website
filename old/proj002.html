Hooligan Baroque

Last match tonight. Home match, and he’s confident. He put his new trainers on. Two-piece tracksuit under his shirt blouse and trenchcoat, he may slip through without being caught. The police are on edge today. They sense what is coming, and are desperate to stop it. He walks through the crowd, spots his boys. They follow him to the stadium. They are quiet; mood is tense but excitable. They’re on their usual side. Game starts. He can see police officers in the corner of his eye. They are yelling, chanting, but holding back still. They have to make it until the end. Game is almost over, but nothing much happened yet.

GOAL

The crowd explodes. Extasy and agression, the fences give in on both sides, the police is blown away, the players are still on the field and are being swallowed by the moment, home versus away versus police versus hooligans. Total chaos and anarchy; a baroque painting.
